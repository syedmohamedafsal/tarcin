Introduction The Project

A maze follower is a type of robot that is designed to navigate through a maze autonomously. The goal of a maze follower robot is to reach the end of the maze as quickly and efficiently as possible, while avoiding walls and obstacles along the way. Maze follower robots use a variety of sensors to detect their surroundings and navigate through the maze. These sensors may include infrared sensors, ultrasonic sensors, or cameras, which provide information about the robot's position and the location of walls and obstacles.

Maze Follower

Maze follower robots are commonly used in educational settings to teach students about robotics, programming, and navigation. They are also used in research and development to explore new algorithms and techniques for autonomous navigation. Overall, maze follower robots are a fascinating and useful application of robotics technology. They demonstrate how robots can be programmed to navigate through complex environments, and they provide a valuable tool for learning and research in the field of robotics.
